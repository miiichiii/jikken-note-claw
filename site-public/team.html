Dispatch flow

How work moves through the room

Mobile-first strip: read top to bottom on phone, left to right on desktop.

01 intake

センセイ → DM

Requests arrive in DM, where macstudio-bot interprets intent and decides whether the task is immediate, light, or delegation-worthy.

02 routing

Triage at deck

If the job is tiny, answer inline. If it needs concentration, a work thread becomes the task room and a sub-agent gets the assignment.

03 execute

Task room work

Heavy coding, research passes, drafting, review loops, and multi-step work are handled outside the main chat so the deck stays uncluttered.

04 return

Back to deck

Sub-agents hand back a concise result. macstudio-bot integrates it and replies cleanly, without leaking messy process details.

Routing policy

Main / orchestrator openai-codex/gpt-5.4

Default control-plane model for intake, synthesis, and final response quality.

Heavy sub-agent work prefers openai/gpt-5.4 gemini-3.1 pro

Use when the job needs depth, independent execution, or a large reasoning budget.

Light / fast tasks gemini-3-flash-preview

Use for quick bounded transformations, triage, short summaries, and lightweight assistive work.

Local AI policy

Local models are not the general default. They are reserved for truly simple, structured, or offline-friendly tasks where minimal reasoning is enough.

Operating lanes

Command Deck Lane

ALWAYS ON

The DM is where the relationship lives. It handles prioritization, confidence checks, approval points, and the final human-facing output.

Sub-agent Lane

HEAVY WORK

Builds, reviews, refactors, long research passes, drafting bursts, and anything likely to bloat the main context get dispatched here.

Flash Lane

LIGHT TASKS

Short, bounded work that benefits from speed over depth routes to the fast model tier rather than the heavy execution path.

Local Utility Lane

STRICTLY SIMPLE

For truly simple or structured tasks only. If judgment, polish, or non-trivial reasoning matters, route back to frontier models instead.
